Scripting, or how to make your computer take care of the boring stuff while you focus on what really matters: napping content

Producing content can be hard regardless of wheter you’re working on a manuscript, a blog post or on a data analysis project. This process involves coming up with your content—which is tough on its own—and a whole bunch of repetitive tasks that are important to your end-product and to future-you. The latter consume your time, your focus, and increase the amount of typing you have to do. Fortunately, you can leave a good portion of these repetitive tasks to someone that doesn’t really mind repetition and happens to be quite good at it: your computer. All you have to do give it some instructions and that’s what you’ll find in this post.

Figure 1: This doesn’t have to be you. Unless you happen to be a cat who likes typing. Even so, it’s your call.

Application #1: Creating a folder structure using bash

The first step in every project is to give it a home in your computer. Creating an informative folder structure makes organizing your files a no-brainer, which is great! It’s also completely mechanical process so let’s put our computer in charge of doing that for us. We can do it using bash.

mkdir data data/raw_data data/tidy_data

mkdir code code/raw_code code/final_code

mkdir figures figures/exploratory figures/explanatory

mkdir products products/writing
